# Body Dysmorphic Disorder (BDD)

## Overview
- **BDD** is a mental health condition involving **excessive preoccupation with perceived physical flaws**, often minor or nonexistent.
- Thoughts and concerns are **persistent, intrusive, and distressing**, significantly impacting daily functioning.
- Prevalence: ~1 in 50 people; affects men and women almost equally. Often begins around **age 12–13**.

---

## Distorted Body Image vs. BDD
- Distorted body image: general dissatisfaction with overall body shape or size.
- BDD: intense focus on a **specific body part** or feature.
- May lead to **excessive grooming, makeup, surgery, or reassurance-seeking**.

---

## Symptoms
Common behaviors and signs include:
- Repeatedly checking appearance in mirrors
- Excessive grooming or use of makeup
- Changing body positions frequently
- Hiding perceived flaws (clothing, accessories)
- Seeking cosmetic surgery or other procedures
- Reassurance-seeking from others
- Skin picking
- Social avoidance and functional impairment

### Impact
- Significant distress affecting **social life, school/work, and overall quality of life**.

---

## Diagnosis Criteria
1. **Preoccupation with perceived defect(s)**, often minor or non-existent.
2. **Repetitive behaviors** to conceal, fix, or respond to the flaw.
3. **Clinically significant distress or impairment** in daily functioning.

---

## Related and Co-occurring Disorders
- **OCD**, Social Anxiety Disorder (SAD), Eating Disorders
- Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD), Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)
- Trichotillomania (hair pulling), Excoriation disorder (skin picking)
- Agoraphobia, Psychotic disorders

---

## Causes
Potential contributing factors:
- Genetic predisposition
- Brain abnormalities
- History of abuse or childhood trauma
- Bullying or social experiences
- Societal and cultural influences (unrealistic beauty standards, social media)
- Triggering events

---

## Commonly Affected Body Parts
- Chest, Hair, Nose or facial features
- Genitalia, Muscularity or size of a body part
- Symmetry of body parts or features

---

## Treatment
### Psychotherapy
- **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)** is first-line.
- Includes **maladaptive thought restructuring** and **exposure techniques** to reduce compulsive behaviors and preoccupation.

### Medications
- **Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs)** can reduce symptoms.
- Most effective when combined with CBT.

### Coping Strategies
- Avoiding social comparison
- Ensuring physical safety (avoid harmful behaviors like excessive skin picking)
- Focusing on **self-acceptance and self-compassion**
- Seeking professional help when needed

---

## Key Takeaways for Chatbot
- Educate users on **BDD vs normal body dissatisfaction**.
- Highlight the role of **repetitive behaviors and reassurance-seeking** in maintaining distress.
- Encourage users to seek **professional diagnosis and treatment**.
- Provide guidance on **coping strategies and self-acceptance**.
