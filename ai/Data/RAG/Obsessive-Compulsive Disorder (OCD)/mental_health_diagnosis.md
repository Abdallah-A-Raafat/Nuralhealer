# Mental Health Diagnosis, Symptoms, Types, and Treatments

## Key Points
- Mental health diagnosis involves noticing changes in **thinking, feeling, or behavior**.  
- **1 in 5 U.S. adults** experiences mental illness each year.  
- Mental illnesses are **common and treatable**, and support is available.  
- Diagnosis reflects **distress or functional impairment** in social, work, or family life.  

---

## What is Mental Illness?
- Defined by the American Psychiatric Association as changes in **emotion, thinking, or behavior**.  
- **Mental health**: ability to function effectively in daily life.  
- **Mental illness**: diagnosable conditions that impair functioning and cause distress.  
- Early treatment is important for **daily functioning and well-being**.  

---

## Prevalence
- **1 in 5 U.S. adults** experience mental illness yearly.  
- **1 in 25 adults** live with serious mental illness (SMI), e.g., bipolar disorder, schizophrenia.  
- **1 in 6 youth aged 6–17** experience mental illness.  

---

## Types of Mental Health Conditions
- **Anxiety Disorders:** GAD, Panic Disorder, Social Anxiety Disorder  
- **Bipolar & Related Disorders:** Bipolar I, Bipolar II, Cyclothymia  
- **Depressive Disorders:** Major Depression, PMDD  
- **Disruptive, Impulse-Control, & Conduct Disorders:** ODD, Kleptomania, Pyromania  
- **Dissociative Disorders**  
- **Elimination Disorders**  
- **Feeding & Eating Disorders:** Anorexia, Bulimia, Binge-Eating  
- **Gender Dysphoria**  
- **Neurocognitive Disorders:** Alzheimer’s, Huntington’s, TBI  
- **Neurodevelopmental Disorders:** ADHD, Autism, Learning Disabilities  
- **Obsessive-Compulsive & Related Disorders:** OCD, Hoarding, Body Dysmorphic  
- **Paraphilic Disorders**  
- **Personality Disorders** (10 recognized types)  
- **Schizophrenia Spectrum & Other Psychotic Disorders**  
- **Sexual Dysfunctions**  
- **Sleep-Wake Disorders:** Insomnia, Narcolepsy  
- **Somatic Symptom & Related Disorders**  
- **Substance-Related & Addictive Disorders**  
- **Trauma & Stressor-Related Disorders:** PTSD  

---

## Signs and Symptoms
- Excessive fear or uneasiness  
- Mood changes: sadness, irritability, hopelessness  
- Problems thinking: memory, concentration, or speech issues  
- Sleep or appetite changes  
- Withdrawal from activities or people  

> Presence of one or two symptoms does not confirm a diagnosis but may indicate **need for evaluation**.

---

## Causes of Mental Illness
- **Biology:** Brain chemistry and neurotransmitter imbalances  
- **Environmental exposures:** prenatal exposure to substances/toxins  
- **Genetics:** family history of mental illness  
- **Life experiences:** trauma, attachment issues, or chronic stress  

---

## Diagnosis Process
1. **Physical Exam:** Rule out physical causes like thyroid problems.  
2. **Psychological Evaluation:** Interviews, family input, symptom tracking, DSM-5 criteria.  
3. **Provisional vs. Differential Diagnosis:** Sometimes more than one mental illness is present.  

---

## Treatment
- Mental illnesses are **treatable**, though not always curable.  
- May include **medication, talk therapy, or combination therapy**.  
- Complementary and alternative therapies can help in some conditions.  
- Treatment often requires **trial and error** to find the best approach.  

---

## Getting Help
- Contact a **physician or mental health professional** for assessment.  
- Psychotherapists can provide direct evaluation and treatment.  
- **SAMHSA National Helpline:** 1-800-662-4357 for resources and support.  

---

## Practical Advice for Chatbot
- Explain what **mental illness is vs. mental health**.  
- Guide users on **common symptoms** and when to seek professional help.  
- Emphasize that **early intervention improves outcomes**.  
- Provide **support resources** and helplines for urgent situations.
