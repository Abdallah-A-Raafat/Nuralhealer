# Mental Illness Screening and Testing

## Key Points
- **No single test** can diagnose a mental illness or determine full mental health.  
- Diagnosis is made by a **medical or mental health professional** after a thorough evaluation.  
- **Physical exams and lab tests** are conducted first to rule out other health conditions.  
- Doctors may ask about **personal history, lifestyle, and symptoms** to aid diagnosis.  
- **Screening tests** can identify when further evaluation is needed but do **not diagnose**.  

---

## Reasons to Be Screened
- Avoiding social situations or daily activities  
- Dramatic **mood changes** or swings  
- Persistent anxiety, worry, or fear  
- Feelings of **worthlessness, irritability, or anger**  
- Fatigue or low energy  
- Low mood or sadness  
- Trouble concentrating or coping with stress  
- **Thoughts of self-harm or suicide** — immediate help required  

> For suicidal thoughts: Call **988 (US National Suicide Prevention Lifeline)** or **911** in emergencies.

---

## Diagnosis Process
1. **Physical Exam & Lab Tests**  
   - Rule out medical conditions that mimic mental illness (e.g., hypothyroidism causing depression-like symptoms).  

2. **Personal History**  
   - Covers marital status, family and social relationships, occupation, and stressors.  

3. **Mental Health Evaluation**  
   - Interviews, questionnaires, and symptom tracking.  
   - Observation of behaviors in office settings.  
   - Uses **DSM-5 criteria** for diagnosis.  

4. **Psychological Tests**  
   - May include:  
     - Beck Depression Inventory (BDI)  
     - Hamilton Anxiety Scale (HAM-A)  
     - Yale-Brown Obsessive Compulsive Scale  
     - Goldberg Bipolar Spectrum Screening Questionnaire  
     - STEP (Schizophrenia & Early Psychosis Indicator)  
   - Assess severity, clarify diagnosis, IQ, learning disabilities, or brain injuries.  

5. **Screening Tests**  
   - Identify signs of common mental disorders:  
     - Anxiety, Depression, ADHD, Bipolar Disorder, PTSD, Eating Disorders, Schizophrenia, Substance/Alcohol Use.  
   - May be administered during annual physicals or online.  
   - Example questions:  
     - Do you feel tired despite enough sleep?  
     - Do you have trouble controlling anger?  
     - Do you rely on substances to cope?  

---

## Online Screening
- **Mental Health America** provides free online screening tools for:  
  - Depression, Anxiety, Bipolar Disorder, Psychosis, Eating Disorders, PTSD, Addiction, and Children-specific screenings.  
- Instant results provide a **score and advice** on next steps.  
- Online tools **do not replace professional evaluation**.  

---

## Getting Help
- Consult a **physician or mental health professional** for proper evaluation.  
- Screening tools can guide you **when to seek professional help**.  
- **Early intervention** is crucial for effective treatment.  
- For support: Call **SAMHSA National Helpline at 1-800-662-4357**.  

---

## Practical Advice for Chatbot
- Explain that **there’s no single mental illness test**; professional evaluation is needed.  
- Guide users on **screening, self-assessment, and warning signs**.  
- Emphasize **emergency resources** for self-harm or suicidal thoughts.  
- Provide information on **next steps**: medical evaluation, online tools, and professional referrals.
