# OCD and Perfectionism

## Overview
- **Perfectionism OCD** (aka "just right" OCD) is a subtype of OCD involving obsessions and compulsions related to making things perfect.
- Leads to **intense checking behaviors** and **anxiety about imperfection**.

---

## What Is Perfectionism?
- **Adaptive/Healthy Perfectionism**: Conscientiousness, goal-directed behavior, good organizational skills, persistence.
- **Maladaptive/Unhealthy Perfectionism**: Distress, low self-esteem, preoccupation with control, past mistakes, fear of new mistakes.

---

## OCD and Perfectionism
- Maladaptive perfectionism is strongly linked to OCD.
- People may feel the need for compulsions to be done “just right” to prevent feared outcomes.
- Often associated with **checking behaviors** and **fear of catastrophic mistakes**.
- Perfectionism can intensify obsessions by increasing monitoring of intrusive thoughts.

---

## Coping Strategies

### Mindfulness
- Reduce investment in thoughts.
- Accept limited control over thoughts.
- Use mindfulness meditation to develop objective awareness of thoughts and emotions.

### Cognitive Behavioral Techniques (CBT)
- **Cognitive restructuring**: Evaluate likelihood/consequences of mistakes.
- **Behavioral experiments**: Challenge unhealthy beliefs and behaviors.
- Helps reduce distress from intrusive thoughts and perfectionistic tendencies.

### Practice Giving Up Control
- Exposure exercises to tolerate loss of control.
- Gradually build confidence in not performing compulsions “just right.”
- Work with a therapist for guidance and feedback.

---

## Living With OCD Perfectionism
- Use self-help strategies for OCD and perfectionism.
- Work on changing unhealthy perfectionistic behaviors.
- Address overachieving tendencies that reinforce OCD symptoms.
- Recognize that OCD manifests differently in each individual.

---

## Key Takeaways
- Perfectionism can maintain or worsen OCD symptoms.
- Coping strategies include mindfulness, CBT, and tolerance-building exercises.
- Professional guidance is recommended for effective management.
- Remission or significant symptom relief is possible for many individuals.
