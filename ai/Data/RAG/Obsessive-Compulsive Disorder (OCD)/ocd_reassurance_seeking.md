# Why Do I Need Constant Reassurance With OCD?

## Overview
- OCD reassurance-seeking is the compulsive need to check repeatedly with someone to ensure everything is okay regarding a worry or obsession.
- While it may seem supportive to respond, it reinforces OCD behaviors and thoughts.
- Excessive reassurance-seeking can harm relationships, promote avoidance, and validate obsessive thoughts.

---

## Examples of OCD Reassurance-Seeking

1. **Safety**
   - Obsessed with causing harm (e.g., hitting someone while driving).
   - Repeatedly asks a loved one to confirm safety.

2. **Sexual Thoughts**
   - Intrusive thoughts about harming someone sexually.
   - Continuously asks family/friends if these thoughts make them a bad person.

3. **Health**
   - Obsessed with contracting diseases (e.g., from public doorknobs).
   - Seeks repeated confirmation that hands are clean or safe.

4. **Death**
   - Fears that a loved one might die in an accident.
   - Frequently calls or checks on the loved one to ensure safety.

---

## How Reassurance Backfires

- **Harms relationships**: Family/friends withdraw, increasing stress.
- **Promotes avoidance**: Reinforces inability to tolerate uncertainty.
- **Validates the obsession**: Strengthens the perceived need for reassurance.

---

## Providing Helpful Support

1. **Agree to Stop**
   - Recognize excessive reassurance-seeking as a compulsion.
   - Families and individuals commit to reducing or stopping this behavior.

2. **Target the Main Issues**
   - Identify common reassurance triggers and write down typical responses.
   - Use cards or notes instead of asking directly to reduce distress and improve relationships.

- **Teach strategies for uncertainty**: Encourage learning to tolerate uncertainty rather than relying on reassurance.

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is reassurance seeking a symptom of?**  
- A symptom of OCD used to reduce anxiety and perceived threat.

**Q: What type of person needs constant reassurance?**  
- Primarily people with OCD, but also those with anxiety or dependent personality disorder.

**Q: How do I stop excessive reassurance seeking?**  
- **Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP)**: Gradual exposure to triggers while preventing reassurance-seeking to reduce fear and compulsions over time.
