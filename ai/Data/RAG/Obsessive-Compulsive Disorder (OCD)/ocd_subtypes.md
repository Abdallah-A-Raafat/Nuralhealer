# OCD Subtypes: Types of Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder

## Overview
- OCD presents in many ways; symptoms vary widely between individuals.
- DSM-5-TR defines OCD as presence of obsessions and/or compulsions causing major distress or disruption.
- There are several **main OCD subtypes** as well as other related subtypes.

---

## Main OCD Subtypes

1. **Contamination obsessions with cleaning/washing compulsions**
   - Intense anxiety about germs or dirt.
   - Excessive washing or cleaning to relieve distress.

2. **Harm obsessions with checking compulsions**
   - Fear of causing harm to self or others.
   - Compulsive checking behaviors to prevent perceived harm.

3. **Obsessions without visible compulsions (Pure O)**
   - Intrusive thoughts about sexual, religious, or aggressive themes.
   - Mental rituals like counting, reciting, or praying to relieve anxiety.

4. **Symmetry obsessions with ordering/arranging/counting compulsions**
   - Strong need for things to be “just right.”
   - May include arranging objects or repeating words until perfect.

5. **Hoarding**
   - Collection of items with little value; living spaces become cluttered.
   - Often associated with anxiety, depression, and functional impairment.

---

## Other OCD Subtypes

- **Relationship OCD**: Obsessions and compulsions related to romantic relationships (e.g., doubts about partner's love).
- **“Just Right” OCD**: Compulsions related to correcting feelings that things are not right.
- **False Memory OCD**: Doubts about past actions, e.g., whether you accidentally stole something.
- **Magical Thinking OCD**: Belief that failure to perform certain behaviors causes unrelated negative events.

---

## Obsessive-Compulsive Related Disorders

- **Skin picking disorder (Excoriation)**
- **Hair-pulling disorder (Trichotillomania)**
- **Body dysmorphic disorder** (including muscle dysmorphia)
- **Substance/medication or medical-condition induced OCD**
- **Postpartum OCD**: Increased risk after childbirth for onset or worsening of symptoms.

---

## OCD in Children

- Childhood OCD may have similarities to adult OCD, but with differences in symptoms and treatment.
- **PANDAS**: Pediatric autoimmune neuropsychiatric disorders associated with streptococcal infections can trigger sudden OCD or tic disorders after strep infection.

---

## Treatment for OCD Subtypes

- Treatment depends on:
  - Past responses
  - Attitudes toward medication
  - Motivation for **ERP** or **CBT**
  - Severity of symptoms
  - Presence of comorbidities
- Most OCD types respond to a combination of **CBT**, **exposure-response prevention (ERP)**, and sometimes medication.
- **Hoarding** may not respond well to medication but can improve with psychotherapy.
