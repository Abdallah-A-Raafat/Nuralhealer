# The Link Between OCD and Suicide

## Key Takeaways
- People with OCD are 10 times more likely to die by suicide than the general population.
- Warning signs include talking about death, feeling hopeless, and giving away possessions.
- If you are having suicidal thoughts, contact the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 988 or call 911 if in immediate danger.

---

## OCD and Suicide
- OCD is a chronic mental illness often associated with disability and suffering.
- People with OCD may experience relationship difficulties, work problems, and feelings of hopelessness.
- Suicide risk is higher for those with OCD, especially when combined with depression or other mental health issues.

### Factors Increasing Suicide Risk in OCD
- Severity of OCD symptoms
- Co-occurring depression
- Feelings of hopelessness
- Presence of personality disorders (e.g., OCPD)
- History of self-harm
- Substance use
- Unemployment or social isolation

---

## Potential Suicide Warning Signs
1. **Increased hopelessness:** Talking about feeling helpless or that they "can't take it anymore."
2. **Talking about death or suicide:** Open discussions about dying or suicidal thoughts.
3. **Increased depression:** Withdrawal, crying, loss of interest, disrupted sleep, and poor appetite.
4. **Preparing for death:** Adjusting wills, insurance policies, or sharing final wishes.
5. **Behavior changes:** Reckless or impulsive acts, or sudden unexplained cheerfulness.
6. **Giving away possessions:** Distributing prized items to friends or family.

---

## How to Help Someone With OCD at Risk
### Keep Communicating
- Talk openly about feelings; discussing suicide does not increase risk.
- Express your own emotions, including worry and fear.

### Ask Questions
- Ask directly about suicidal thoughts, plans, and methods.
- Assess access to means (weapons, medications, etc.).

### Empathize, Don't Minimize
- Avoid telling them to "get over it" or "think positively."
- Show understanding and acknowledge the difficulty of their experience.

---

## Getting Professional Help
- Suicide ideation may require hospitalization and professional intervention.
- In immediate danger, accompany the person to the emergency department or wait for authorities.
- In less urgent cases, help them access:
  - National Suicide Prevention Lifeline (988)
  - Mental health professionals
  - Support groups
