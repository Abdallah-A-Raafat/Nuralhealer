# Pure Obsessional OCD (Pure O)

## Overview
- Pure O is a subtype of **OCD** marked by intrusive, unwanted, uncontrollable thoughts (obsessions).
- Unlike classic OCD, compulsions are mostly **mental rituals** rather than outward behaviors.
- Common misconception: Pure O is "less severe" — in reality, obsessions can be very distressing and disruptive.

---

## Symptoms

### Obsessions
- Recurrent, persistent, intrusive thoughts, images, or urges.
- Can involve:
  - Aggression
  - Sexual themes
  - Religious concerns
  - Symmetry or contamination

### Compulsions
- Mental rituals performed to reduce distress:
  - Mentally reviewing memories or information
  - Mentally repeating words
  - Mentally undoing or redoing actions
- Reassurance-seeking behaviors:
  - Asking others for assurance
  - Avoiding anxiety-provoking situations
  - Searching for information online
- Compulsions in Pure O are **less visible but still compulsive**.

---

## Causes and Risk Factors
- **Biological factors:** Brain circuits filtering thoughts may function differently.
- **Family history:** Higher prevalence in those with relatives with OCD.
- **Genetics:** Variations in specific genes may contribute.

---

## Subtypes Related to Pure O
- **Harm OCD:** Fears of causing harm to self or others.
- **Pedophilia OCD (pOCD):** Intrusive sexual thoughts about children with possible mental rituals.
- **Relationship OCD (ROCD):** Obsessive doubts about relationships or sexual attraction.
- **Sexual orientation OCD (SO-OCD / HOCD):** Extreme anxiety about sexual orientation.

---

## Treatment

### Psychotherapy
- **Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT)** is effective.
- Must address **underlying mental rituals** for complete efficacy.

### Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP)
- Behavioral therapy helping patients face obsessions without performing compulsions.
- Increases short-term distress but improves long-term symptom control.

### Medication
- **SSRIs** (Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors)
- **Clomipramine (Anafranil)** – tricyclic antidepressant
- **Atypical antipsychotics** may augment SSRI effects.
- ~60% respond to SSRIs, with 40–50% symptom reduction.

---

## Self-Help Strategies
- **Relaxation techniques:** Deep breathing, mindfulness, progressive muscle relaxation.
- **Exercise:** Aerobic activity may reduce OCD symptoms.
- **Support groups:** Online or in-person groups for Pure O support and information.

---

## Key Takeaways
- Pure O involves **intrusive obsessions and hidden mental compulsions**.
- Symptoms can significantly interfere with daily life.
- Professional help (therapy and/or medication) is recommended for effective management.
- Talking about distressing thoughts is the **first step to relief**.
