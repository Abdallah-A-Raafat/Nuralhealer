# Identifying and Coping With Relationship OCD

## Key Takeaways
- Relationship OCD (ROCD) involves intrusive thoughts and compulsive behaviors about your partner and relationship.
- Psychotherapy and medication can help manage ROCD symptoms.
- Open and honest communication with your partner is essential to building trust and managing OCD.

---

## What is Relationship OCD?
- ROCD is a form of OCD focused on romantic relationships.
- Causes repetitive thoughts centered on doubts or fears about the relationship.
- Can lead to compulsive behaviors seeking reassurance from the partner.
- Creates stress for both the person with OCD and the relationship itself.

---

## Symptoms of ROCD
- Intrusive thoughts about your relationship or partner
- Constant worry about whether your partner truly loves you
- Excessive concern about partner’s happiness or well-being
- Overthinking partner’s flaws
- Wondering if you could have found a “better” partner
- Repeatedly seeking reassurance
- Feeling distracted and unable to focus

> For a diagnosis, these obsessions and compulsions must interfere with normal functioning.

---

## Triggers of Relationship OCD
Factors that may increase risk:
- Brain activity changes
- Difficulties in close relationships
- History of abuse or trauma
- Loss of a loved one
- Major life changes (moving, marriage)
- Changes in serotonin levels
- Anxious attachment style, poor self-esteem, past negative experiences

---

## Types of Relationship OCD
1. **Relationship-focused:** Obsessions about the relationship itself, e.g., “Does my partner really love me?”
2. **Partner-focused:** Obsessions about the partner’s characteristics, e.g., personality, intelligence, or other traits.

---

## How ROCD Feels
- Challenges maintaining self-esteem and intimate relationships
- Constant reassurance-seeking from partner can cause frustration
- Secrecy about obsessions and compulsions may impede trust
- Can interfere with sexual intimacy and lead to dissatisfaction

---

## Treatment of ROCD
### Psychotherapy
- Helps manage OCD symptoms and address low self-esteem, poor social skills, assertiveness, and confidence.
- Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP) can be effective.

### Medication
- Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs) may reduce OCD symptoms.
- Treatment plans should be discussed with a doctor or mental health professional.

---

## Coping Strategies
1. **Involve Your Partner**
   - Include your partner in therapy sessions if possible.
   - Helps build understanding and trust.

2. **Maintain Open Communication**
   - Share your experiences and symptoms honestly.
   - Prevents misunderstandings and strengthens intimacy.

3. **Join a Support Group**
   - Provides social support and reduces feelings of isolation.
   - Follow group rules to maintain confidentiality.

---

## Recap
- Treating OCD reduces its impact on relationships.
- Self-help strategies: honest communication, involving partner, and support groups.
